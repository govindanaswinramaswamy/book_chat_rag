Batch Processing Versus Stream Processing
Once your data arrives in data storage engines like databases, data lakes, or data
warehouses, it becomes historical data. This is opposed to streaming data (data that
is still streaming in). Historical data is often processed in batch jobs—jobs that are
kicked off periodically. For example, once a day, you might want to kick off a batch
job to compute the average surge charge for all the rides in the last day.
When data is processed in batch jobs, we refer to it as batch processing. Batch process!
ing has been a research subject for many decades, and companies have come up with
distributed systems like MapReduce and Spark to process batch data efficiently.

we say that you have streaming data. Stream processing refers to doing computation
